Types of Free Climbing Disciplines

Sport Climbing

Sport climbing involves climbing routes that are protected by pre-placed bolts drilled into the rock. Climbers clip quickdraws into these bolts as they ascend. This discipline emphasizes athletic movement, endurance, and technical skill, allowing climbers to focus on challenging sequences without worrying about placing protection.

Bouldering

Bouldering is performed on shorter walls or natural boulders, typically up to 5 meters high, without the use of ropes. Climbers use crash pads for protection. Bouldering focuses on powerful, technical moves and problem-solving, making it accessible and social, often practiced indoors and outdoors.

LEARN MORE ABOUT BOULDERING BOULDERING GEAR

Trad Climbing (Traditional Climbing)

Trad climbing requires climbers to place their own removable protection (such as cams and nuts) into cracks and features as they ascend. This discipline demands a strong understanding of gear placement, route finding, and self-reliance, offering a sense of adventure and exploration.

LEARN MORE ABOUT TRAD CLIMBING TRAD-CLIMBING GEAR

Deep Water Solo Climbing

Deep water soloing is a form of free climbing performed above deep water, which acts as the safety net in case of a fall. No ropes or harnesses are used. This discipline combines the thrill of solo climbing with the safety of a water landing, often practiced on sea cliffs or overhanging rocks.

LEARN MORE ABOUT DEEP WATER SOLOING DWS GEAR

Free Solo Climbing

Free soloing is the most extreme form of rock climbing, performed without any ropes, harnesses, or protective equipment. The climber relies solely on their physical and mental abilities, with no margin for error. This discipline represents the purest but most dangerous form of climbing, where any mistake can be fatal.
